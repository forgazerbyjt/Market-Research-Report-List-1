<p><p>Digital music distribution services operate through two primary market types: indirect and direct distribution. Indirect distribution involves intermediaries, such as record labels and digital aggregators, who manage the distribution of music to various platforms, providing artists with wider reach and marketing support. In contrast, direct distribution allows artists to upload and sell their music directly to consumers through platforms like Bandcamp or SoundCloud, enabling greater control over pricing and audience engagement without intermediaries.</p></p>
