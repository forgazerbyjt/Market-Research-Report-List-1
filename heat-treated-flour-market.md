<p><p>The heat-treated flour market can be categorized into two primary types: dry and wet. Dry heat-treated flour undergoes a process involving high temperatures without moisture, enhancing shelf-life and reducing microbial activity. In contrast, wet heat-treated flour is subjected to steam or moisture, which modifies its functional properties and improves dough performance. Both types are used in various applications, including baking and food processing, catering to different consumer needs and preferences within the food industry.</p></p>
