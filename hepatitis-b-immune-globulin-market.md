<p><p>The Hepatitis B Immune Globulin market is segmented into two primary administration types: intravenous injection and intramuscular injection. Intravenous injection is used for rapid delivery and immediate immune response, typically in severe cases or during acute infections. In contrast, intramuscular injection is the more common route, offering a slower, sustained release of antibodies, which is ideal for post-exposure prophylaxis and vaccination scenarios. Both methods ensure effective prevention and treatment of Hepatitis B through the provision of passive immunity.</p></p>
