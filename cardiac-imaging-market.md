<p><ul><li>Coronary Catheterization</li><li>Echocardiogram</li><li>Intravascular Ultrasound</li><li>Positron Emission Tomography</li><li>Computed Tomography</li><li>Magnetic Resonance Angiography</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cardiac imaging market includes various techniques for diagnosing heart conditions. Coronary catheterization provides detailed imaging of coronary arteries using a catheter. Echocardiograms use ultrasound to examine heart structure and function. Intravascular ultrasound offers internal views of blood vessels. Positron emission tomography (PET) assesses blood flow and metabolic activity in the heart. Computed tomography (CT) creates cross-sectional images for coronary artery evaluation. Magnetic resonance angiography (MRA) visualizes blood vessels and assesses heart anatomy without radiation exposure. Each method serves distinct diagnostic needs.</p></p>
