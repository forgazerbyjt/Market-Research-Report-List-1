Water-cooled capacitors utilize liquid cooling systems to efficiently dissipate heat, making them suitable for high-power applications. Air-cooled capacitors rely on ambient air for cooling, offering simplicity and lower maintenance, which is ideal for smaller systems. Each type serves distinct operational needs.</p></p>
